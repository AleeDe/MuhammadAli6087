What is HCI? (Page# 3–4)

In this course-book framing, HCI is treated as a central concern in systems design. It involves the design, implementation, and evaluation of interactive systems in the context of the user’s task and work.

“User” can mean an individual, a group working together, or multiple users in an organization. So HCI is not limited to a single person using a desktop computer.

Short-Term Memory and Chunking (Page# 29–30)

The book discusses short-term memory capacity often summarized as 7 ± 2 items. A more practical idea is that people can remember roughly 7 ± 2 chunks, where a chunk is a meaningful grouping of information.

Design implication: group related information into chunks so users don’t have to memorize long, unstructured lists.

Fitts’ Law: size and distance affect target selection time (Chapter 1)

The book explains that the time to hit a target depends on the distance to the target and the target’s size. Design implication: important controls should be easier to hit (larger) and easier to reach (closer).
